# Creator Economy Failure Scenarios

These scenarios support the paper's threat model. They are plausible examples, not claims about specific real cases unless cited in the paper.

## Fake Sponsorship

A synthetic ad shows a known creator endorsing a supplement, course, crypto project, beauty product, or investment platform. The creator never agreed. The ad borrows the creator's audience trust, converts that trust into sales, and disappears before enforcement catches up.

## Synthetic Apology

A realistic video appears to show a creator admitting wrongdoing. Reaction accounts amplify it before verification. Even if removed, the fake becomes the first story the creator has to fight.

## Reality Denial

A creator posts real footage of harassment, unsafe working conditions, a public incident, or abuse. The accused party calls it AI generated. The audience splits before the creator can prove origin.

## Fake Eyewitness Event

A video appears to show an emergency at a stadium, concert, protest, school, or airport. Local creators repost it because it looks urgent. When it is later proven fake, the next real emergency becomes harder to believe.

## Synthetic Expertise

An AI persona becomes trusted in cooking, health, parenting, finance, fitness, or beauty. At first it seems harmless. Then the persona sells courses, recommends products, gives advice, or builds emotional dependency.

## Private Community Scam

A creator's voice and writing style are cloned to invite fans into a fake investment group, paid community, or giveaway. The scam moves through direct messages, external sites, and payment tools.

## Nonconsensual Deepfake Abuse

A creator discovers intimate synthetic media and must navigate slow reporting channels, unclear evidence requirements, and cross platform reposting. The harm is personal, reputational, and occupational.

## AI Content Farm

Networks of synthetic accounts produce low cost content across finance, celebrity, history, news, and health. They occupy search results and recommendation slots, weakening trust in the whole category.

## Why Capture Proof Helps

Capture proof does not solve every scenario. It does not remove all fakes or prevent all abuse.

It does create a verified reference point. A creator can point to a signed original, a public verification page, and a visible origin record. That changes the burden of proof from pure reputation to inspectable evidence.
