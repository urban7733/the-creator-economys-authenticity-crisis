# Apex Verify Case Study

Project link: https://www.apexverify.tech/

Apex Verify is used in this repository as a case study for capture first authenticity infrastructure in the creator economy.

## Why It Matters

Most platform responses to AI content happen after upload. A platform may look for metadata, ask the creator to self disclose AI use, run a detection model, or wait for user reports.

That is too late for many creator economy risks:

- metadata can be stripped,
- screenshots can erase provenance,
- videos can be reuploaded,
- labels can disappear,
- detector performance can fall as generation models improve,
- and real footage can still be dismissed as fake.

Apex Verify starts from the opposite premise: if the claim is that something real happened, the proof should begin at the moment of capture.

## Capture First Workflow

The case study model is:

1. The creator captures photo or video inside a controlled app flow.
2. The media is signed at the source.
3. Authenticity data is attached through C2PA style provenance.
4. Public posts and creator profiles expose verification context.
5. Private sharing can still provide time limited links when the creator does not want a permanent public post.

This approach does not prove that every unverified post is fake. It creates a positive proof path for media that claims to be real.

## Design Position

Apex Verify is not framed here as a detector. It is a trust layer.

Detection asks: "Does this look fake?"

Capture proof asks: "Can this media show where it came from?"

Both layers matter, but they solve different problems. Detection helps platforms respond to abuse. Capture proof helps creators preserve credibility before abuse happens.

## Creator Economy Relevance

Creators need verification in situations where audiences expect reality:

- breaking news,
- live events,
- public incidents,
- product testing,
- travel and venue content,
- journalism,
- health and fitness proof,
- marketplace trust,
- brand sponsorships,
- and creator identity disputes.

In lower stakes categories, such as fictional art, logo generation, background visuals, or clearly synthetic entertainment, audiences may not need the same level of proof. The key is context.
